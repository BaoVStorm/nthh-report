# Reflection Report “AWS First Cloud AI Journey Community Day 2026”

### Event Information

* **Event name:** AWS First Cloud AI Journey Community Day 2026
* **Time:** Saturday, March 21, 2026 | 09:00 – 12:00
* **Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower - 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City
* **Main topics:** Cloud, Platform Engineering, DevOps, Generative AI, Agentic AI, Multimodal GenAI, and Amazon CloudFront

### Event Objectives

* Connect the community of students, young engineers, and experts in the fields of Cloud, DevOps, and Generative AI.
* Introduce new trends in building modern engineering platforms, especially Platform Engineering and Agentic AI.
* Share real-world applications of Generative AI on AWS through topics such as GenAIOps, Multimodal GenAI, GraphRAG, and Multi-Agent Workflows.
* Provide insights into career pathways in Cloud, DevOps, Platform Engineering, and AI Engineering.
* Mark the official kickoff of FCAJ Bootcamp 2026, beginning a learning journey for students to develop Cloud skills.

* **Phat Pham** - Software Engineer, Katalon
* **Phap Nguyen** - Cloud Engineer, VPBank

### Key Highlights

#### Building Modern Platform Engineering & Career Pathways

The first session of the event provided an overview of **Platform Engineering** and its role in the modern Cloud and DevOps ecosystem. The content did not only focus on technical aspects but also covered company culture, internship opportunities, and career orientation for students and young engineers.

Some key points included:

* Introducing how Platform Engineering helps developers build, deploy, and operate systems more effectively.
* Clarifying the role of Platform Engineering in Cloud, DevOps, and modern software systems.
* Sharing insights about company culture, internship opportunities, and how students can prepare the necessary skills to enter the technology industry.
* Organizing an open Q&A session, allowing participants to interact directly with industry experts.

#### GenAIOps Essential - DevOps for Generative AI Applications

This session focused on **GenAIOps**, which refers to applying DevOps thinking to the development and operation of Generative AI applications. This is an important topic because modern AI systems do not only require good models, but also need the ability to be deployed, monitored, optimized, and operated reliably in real-world environments.

Some key highlights included:

* Presenting real-world examples of GenAIOps in AWS projects.
* Introducing tools and services such as **Bedrock AgentCore Observability**, **Amazon EKS**, and **Langfuse**.
* Emphasizing the role of observability in monitoring, evaluating, and improving the quality of GenAI applications.

#### Shipping Code in the Agentic Era

This session focused on how the software development process is changing in the era of **Agentic AI**. As AI tools become increasingly capable of supporting programming, analysis, and automation, software engineers need to adapt their workflows to make better use of these tools.

Some key points included:

* Analyzing common problems in the software development and deployment process.
* Introducing tools that support developers in the Agentic AI era.
* Presenting a deep dive to clarify how AI can support the coding workflow.
* Providing a productivity playbook to help engineers improve work efficiency.
* Demonstrating practical examples to help participants better understand how AI can be applied to the software development process.

#### Production-Grade Multimodal GenAI on AWS

This session focused on building Generative AI applications that can be deployed in production environments. The content went beyond chatbots or text generation and expanded into multimodal search, GraphRAG, multi-agent workflows, and requirements for safety and system observability.

Some key highlights included:

* Introducing the architecture of next-generation AI applications.
* Presenting how to build **Multimodal Search** using **Nova Embeddings**.
* Introducing **GraphRAG** for enterprise knowledge extraction and utilization.
* Presenting the **Multi-Agent Workflows** model, where multiple AI agents collaborate to handle complex tasks.
* Emphasizing safety, observability, and control when deploying GenAI in real-world environments.

### Key Takeaways

#### Mindset About Cloud and Platform Engineering

* I gained a clearer understanding that Platform Engineering is an important development direction in the Cloud and DevOps ecosystem.
* I realized that a good engineering platform can help developers deploy products faster, reduce manual work, and improve system stability.
* I learned more about the skills needed to pursue positions related to Cloud, DevOps, Platform Engineering, or AI Engineering.
* I understood that a career path in Cloud does not only require knowledge of AWS services, but also systems thinking, automation capability, and operational skills.

#### Knowledge About GenAIOps

* When GenAI is applied in real-world scenarios, it needs to be operated like a production-grade software system.
* Factors such as deployment, monitoring, logging, observability, and security are very important in Generative AI applications.
* Tools such as Bedrock AgentCore Observability, EKS, and Langfuse can support monitoring, evaluation, and optimization of AI systems.
* Applying DevOps to GenAI makes systems easier to control, reduces risks, and improves application quality.

#### Software Development Mindset in the Agentic AI Era

* AI is changing the way software engineers write code, test, debug, and deploy products.
* Developers need to know how to use AI as a supporting tool instead of depending on it completely.
* A productivity playbook can help optimize workflows, improve efficiency, and reduce time spent on repetitive tasks.
* In the Agentic AI era, important skills are not only about writing code, but also about defining problems, verifying results, and controlling output quality.

#### Knowledge About Multimodal GenAI

* Multimodal GenAI expands AI capabilities from text processing to other data types such as images, documents, and enterprise knowledge.
* Nova Embeddings can be used to build multimodal search systems.
* GraphRAG improves enterprise knowledge retrieval and utilization by combining graph-based approaches with retrieval-augmented generation.
* Multi-Agent Workflows are a new approach that allows multiple AI agents to collaborate on complex tasks.
* When deploying GenAI into production, it is especially important to pay attention to safety, observability, and quality control.

### Applying to Study and Work

* Apply Platform Engineering thinking to AWS projects by building clear, repeatable, and operable deployment workflows.
* When working on Cloud projects, pay more attention to monitoring, logging, and observability instead of only focusing on successful deployment.
* Apply GenAIOps thinking when building AI applications using AWS, especially systems that need to monitor output quality and performance.
* When developing software, use AI tools to support coding, error checking, system explanation, and learning productivity.
* Knowledge about Multimodal GenAI, GraphRAG, and Multi-Agent Workflows can serve as a direction for expanding AI projects in the future.

### Event Experience

Participating in **“AWS First Cloud AI Journey Community Day 2026”** was a very valuable experience that gave me the opportunity to access new knowledge about Cloud, DevOps, Platform Engineering, and Generative AI. The event was not only a knowledge-sharing session but also a space for connecting students, the technology community, and industry experts.

#### Learning from highly skilled speakers

The presentations were arranged according to a reasonable timeline, starting with Platform Engineering and then expanding to GenAIOps, Agentic AI, and Multimodal GenAI. As a result, I gained a more comprehensive view of how modern technologies are combined to build real-world software systems.

Through the speakers’ sharing, I understood more clearly that Cloud is not only a place to store or deploy applications, but also a foundation for building systems that are scalable, observable, optimized, and stable in operation.

#### Hands-on technical exposure

One of the highlights of the event was the content related to Generative AI and Agentic AI. Previously, I often viewed GenAI from the perspective of chatbots or question-answering support tools. However, after the event, I understood that when GenAI is applied in practice, it requires an entire ecosystem behind it, including cloud infrastructure, DevOps, observability, security, and operational processes.

Topics such as GenAIOps, GraphRAG, Multimodal Search, and Multi-Agent Workflows helped me realize that modern AI applications need to be well-designed, controllable, and suitable for real business needs.

#### Strengthening My Learning and Career Direction

The session on **Building Modern Platform Engineering & Career Pathways** helped me gain a clearer direction on the skills I need to develop in the Cloud field. Besides knowledge of AWS services, learners also need to improve systems thinking, automation skills, operational skills, and the ability to learn new technologies.

The event also helped me better understand internship opportunities, how to connect with the community, and how to prepare myself for roles related to Cloud, DevOps, and AI Engineering.

#### Networking and discussions

Besides the knowledge gained, the event was also a good opportunity to network with the community, meet people who are interested in AWS, and interact directly with experts. The event atmosphere was dynamic, professional, and inspiring, giving me more motivation to continue learning and developing my skills in the Cloud field.

#### Lessons Learned

* Cloud, DevOps, and AI are becoming increasingly connected in modern systems.
* To apply Generative AI in real-world scenarios, a stable operating infrastructure with observability and control is required.
* Platform Engineering is an important direction that helps optimize developer experience and improve software deployment efficiency.
* Agentic AI does not completely replace developers, but becomes a supporting tool that helps increase productivity and solve more complex problems.
* When building systems on AWS, it is necessary to consider performance, security, cost, scalability, and reliability.
